5. Good Leaver / Bad Leaver

5.1 Good Leaver

A Shareholder is a "Good Leaver" if they cease to be involved due to: (a) death or permanent incapacity; (b) termination by the Company without cause; or (c) mutual written agreement. A Good Leaver retains all vested shares; unvested shares transfer to the Company at nil value.

5.2 Bad Leaver

A Shareholder is a "Bad Leaver" if they cease due to: (a) resignation without 3 months' written notice; (b) dismissal for gross misconduct, fraud, or material breach; or (c) breach of non-compete or confidentiality obligations. A Bad Leaver must transfer all shares (vested and unvested) at the lower of cost price or fair market value.

6. Pre-Emption Rights

6.1 Right of First Refusal

Before transferring shares to a third party, a Shareholder must first offer those shares to the other Shareholders pro-rata at the same price and terms. Non-selling Shareholders have 30 days to accept.

6.2 New Share Issuances

Any new shares shall first be offered to existing Shareholders on a pro-rata basis before being offered to third parties.

7. Drag-Along Rights

If Shareholders holding more than 75% of shares accept a bona fide third-party offer to acquire all shares, they may require remaining Shareholders to sell on the same terms and price.

8. Tag-Along Rights

If any Shareholder proposes to sell more than 20% of total shares, other Shareholders may require the buyer to also purchase a pro-rata portion of their shares on the same terms (exercisable within 21 days).

9. Intellectual Property

All IP created in connection with the Company's business is the exclusive property of the Company. Each Shareholder irrevocably assigns all such IP and agrees to execute further documents to perfect such assignment. Pre-existing IP used in the business is licensed royalty-free and perpetually to the Company.

10. Confidentiality

Each Shareholder shall keep confidential all information relating to the Company's business, technology, finances, strategy, and the terms of this Agreement. This obligation survives termination for 3 years. Disclosure is permitted only to professional advisers under equivalent obligations or as required by law.

The parties acknowledge that a separate Non-Disclosure Agreement dated 21 May 2026 exists between them. This clause operates in addition to, and does not supersede, that agreement.

11. Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation

11.1 During Engagement

No Shareholder shall, without Board consent, be engaged in any directly competing business or solicit the Company's customers, partners, or employees.

11.2 Post-Termination (12 months)

For 12 months after cessation of engagement, no Shareholder shall establish or join a directly competing padel matching or social sports application business, or poach the Company's employees, contractors, or key partners.

12. Dividends and Distributions

Dividends may only be declared by Board resolution and shall be paid pro-rata to shareholdings. The Founder may defer his entitlement in writing to fund Company operations.

13. Dispute Resolution

Disputes shall first be resolved by good faith negotiation (30 days), then mediation, before any legal proceedings. This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales, with exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts.
