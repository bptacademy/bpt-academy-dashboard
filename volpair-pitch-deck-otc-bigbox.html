The Problem

Padel is one of the fastest ways
to meet someone new.
Nobody's built the app for that.

Every week, millions of padel players share a court, share a moment, share a post-match drink — and then lose each other in the noise.

Booking platforms do one thing brilliantly: fill courts. But the moment the match ends, the social layer vanishes. There's no way to discover who else is out there — people you've never played with, who might be exactly your type, on and off the court.

  • No way to discover new people you've never played with, matched to you by real data
  • No way to find a compatible doubles partner based on actual play history and skill level
  • No way to turn a shared love of the sport into something more

Tinder, Bumble, Hinge? They match on stated preferences. A profile. A photo. A guess.

Padel booking platforms already have the real data. It's sitting idle.

volpair activates it.

Connect & Play

Two ways in — Connect to meet,
Play to find your partner.

Every volpair user moves through one of two simple, guided flows. Both end the same way: two players, on court, booked through your platform.

💘  CONNECT
The social layer — meet someone new.
1
Discover — see compatible players, ranked by the volpair Score (level, style, availability & intent).
2
Volley — send a private signal. Nobody sees it unless it's mutual — zero rejection by design.
3
Match — when two players Volley each other, it's a match.
4
Serve — a structured first message replaces the blank chat box. No ghosting.
5
Meet on court — arrange a game and book a court on On The Court.
🎾  PLAY
Find a Partner — someone to play with now.
1
Find a Partner — filter nearby players by level, intent & weekly availability.
2
Request — send a play request to a compatible partner.
3
Match — they accept → you're connected.
4
Coordinate — chat to lock a day, time & court.
5
Book & play — book on On The Court → the game feeds the Re-Engagement Engine ↻.
Both flows are live in the app today. Connect builds the relationship; Play turns it into a booking — and every booking flows back through On The Court.

Unit Economics

Healthy economics — even on the conservative line.

LTV (Lifetime Value) = the total revenue an average paying user generates before they cancel.  CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) = what we spend on marketing to win one paying user. A higher LTV : CAC ratio means every £1 of marketing returns more.

MetricConservativeOptimistic
CAC — cost per paying user£50.00£8.33
LTV — value per premium user£165.80£249.63
LTV : CAC ratio3.3 : 130 : 1
Avg premium duration8.3 months12.5 months
Payback period~25 months~2.5 months
Benchmark: LTV:CAC > 3:1 is healthy, > 5:1 excellent. volpair clears the bar at its most cautious assumptions — and OTC referral traffic pushes CAC far lower.
