# MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL**

---

## MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AND NON-CIRCUMVENTION AGREEMENT

**Date:** 11 May 2026

---

## PARTIES

**Party A (Disclosing Party):**

**FABIAN DAVID**, an individual of Romanian nationality, residing at 28 Market Place, Ramsbottom, BL0 9HT, United Kingdom (hereinafter referred to as **"Fabian"** or the **"Founder"**), acting in his personal capacity as the founder of a proposed business venture currently operating under the working name **"Volpair"** (the **"Business"**).

**Party B (Recipient):**

**AIMÉE LAWSON**, an individual residing at 3 Billington Avenue, Rawtenstall, BB4 8UW, United Kingdom (hereinafter referred to as the **"Recipient"**).

Fabian and the Recipient are referred to individually as a **"Party"** and collectively as the **"Parties"**.

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Fabian is developing the Business — a padel-focused social matching and community application and associated platform — and is exploring a potential business relationship and engagement with the Recipient in connection with the Business (the **"Proposed Engagement"**);

WHEREAS, in connection with evaluating and potentially entering into the Proposed Engagement, Fabian may disclose to the Recipient certain confidential and proprietary information relating to the Business;

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to protect such confidential information from unauthorised use and disclosure, and to set out the terms on which such information may be shared;

WHEREAS, it is anticipated that, in due course, the Business may be incorporated into one or more legal entities (tentatively **"Volpair Inc."**, a Delaware C-Corporation, and/or **"Volpair Ltd."**, a UK private limited company), and the Parties agree that this Agreement and the protections herein shall be novated to and assumed by any such entity upon incorporation, by written notice from Fabian to the Recipient;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein, and for other good and valuable consideration (the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged), the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Confidential Information"** means any and all information or data, in whatever form or medium (whether written, oral, electronic, visual or otherwise), that is disclosed by one Party (the **"Disclosing Party"**) to the other Party (the **"Receiving Party"**) in connection with the Proposed Engagement, including but not limited to:

(a) business plans, strategies, financial projections, revenue models, and commercial forecasts;

(b) technology architecture, source code, software, algorithms, product roadmaps, and technical specifications;

(c) customer and user data, partner lists, investor information, and supplier details;

(d) marketing plans, pricing models, partnership terms, and competitive analysis;

(e) intellectual property, trade secrets, inventions (whether patented or not), trademarks, and brand identity materials;

(f) intended corporate structure, equity arrangements, cap table information, and compensation models;

(g) the terms, existence, and subject matter of this Agreement and the Proposed Engagement; and

(h) any other information that is designated as confidential at the time of disclosure or that, by its nature, a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

**1.2 "Permitted Purpose"** means the evaluation of, negotiation of, and (if applicable) participation in the Proposed Engagement between the Parties.

**1.3 "Business"** means the development, operation, and commercialisation of a padel-focused social and matchmaking application and associated platform, as presently being developed by Fabian and as may be further developed, including through any incorporated entity.

---

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF CONFIDENTIALITY

**2.1 Non-Disclosure.** The Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) not disclose, publish, or reveal any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) use the Confidential Information solely for the Permitted Purpose;

(d) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable care; and

(e) immediately notify the Disclosing Party upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected unauthorised use or disclosure of Confidential Information.

**2.2 Restricted Access.** The Receiving Party shall limit access to Confidential Information strictly to those of its own professional advisers who: (i) have a strict need to know such information for the Permitted Purpose; and (ii) are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less protective than those in this Agreement.

**2.3 No Copies.** The Receiving Party shall not copy, reproduce, or summarise Confidential Information except as strictly necessary for the Permitted Purpose.

---

## 3. EXCEPTIONS

**3.1** The obligations in Clause 2 shall not apply to Confidential Information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

(a) was in the public domain at the time of disclosure, or subsequently enters the public domain through no act or omission of the Receiving Party;

(b) was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without restriction;

(c) is rightfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party without restriction and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or

(d) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

**3.2 Compelled Disclosure.** If the Receiving Party is required by law, court order, or regulatory authority to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall: (i) provide prompt prior written notice to the Disclosing Party (to the extent legally permitted); (ii) cooperate with the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate relief; and (iii) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed.

---

## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**4.1 Ownership.** All Confidential Information disclosed by Fabian, and all intellectual property rights therein, shall remain the exclusive property of Fabian (and, upon incorporation, of the relevant Volpair entity). Nothing in this Agreement grants the Recipient any licence, right, or interest in any Confidential Information or intellectual property of Fabian or the Business.

**4.2 No Reverse Engineering.** The Recipient shall not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive source code or underlying ideas from any technical Confidential Information disclosed by Fabian.

**4.3 Work Product.** Any materials, concepts, or work product created by the Recipient specifically as part of a formal engagement under the Proposed Engagement that relate to the Business shall be disclosed to Fabian and shall, to the fullest extent permitted by law, vest in and be the exclusive property of Fabian (or, upon incorporation, the relevant Volpair entity). For the avoidance of doubt, this Clause shall not apply to materials, ideas, or concepts developed by the Recipient independently and outside of any such formal engagement, even if discussed informally during the evaluation phase.

---

## 5. NON-CIRCUMVENTION

**5.1** The Recipient agrees not to, directly or indirectly:

(a) contact, solicit, or enter into any business arrangement with any third party introduced by Fabian in connection with the Business (including but not limited to partners, investors, customers, or service providers) for purposes related to the Business, without Fabian's prior written consent;

(b) use Confidential Information to pursue, establish, or assist any business that competes with the Business; or

(c) circumvent Fabian in any transaction or relationship that arose from or was facilitated by Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement.

**5.2 Pre-Existing Relationships.** For the avoidance of doubt, the restrictions in Clause 5.1 shall not apply to any third party, contact, or relationship that the Recipient can demonstrate was already known to or independently established by the Recipient prior to the date of this Agreement and without reference to any Confidential Information disclosed hereunder.

---

## 6. NON-SOLICITATION

**6.1** During the term of this Agreement and for a period of **twelve (12) months** following its termination or expiry, neither Party shall, without the other Party's prior written consent, solicit, induce, or attempt to solicit or induce any person engaged in connection with the Business to cease or reduce that engagement.

---

## 7. NON-COMPETE (RECIPIENT ONLY)

**7.1** The Recipient acknowledges that Fabian is sharing genuinely sensitive and proprietary information about the Business. In consideration thereof, during the term of this Agreement and for a period of **six (6) months** following termination, the Recipient shall not, without Fabian's prior written consent, directly or indirectly use Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement to establish, assist, or materially contribute to any business that is directly competitive with the Business as defined in Clause 1.3.

**7.2** For the avoidance of doubt, this Clause shall not prevent the Recipient from being employed by, advising, or otherwise engaging with any business in the padel, sports technology, or adjacent sector, provided that the Recipient does not make direct competitive use of Confidential Information received from Fabian under this Agreement.

**7.3** The Recipient acknowledges that this restriction, as narrowed, is reasonable in scope, duration, and geography given the nature and sensitivity of the Confidential Information disclosed.

---

## 8. NOVATION UPON INCORPORATION

**8.1** The Parties agree that upon the incorporation of Volpair Inc. (Delaware) and/or Volpair Ltd. (England and Wales), Fabian may, by written notice to the Recipient, novate and assign all of his rights and obligations under this Agreement to the relevant incorporated entity. The Recipient hereby consents in advance to such novation and agrees to execute any further document reasonably required to give effect to it.

**8.2** Such novation shall not diminish or alter the Recipient's obligations under this Agreement.

---

## 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

**9.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the date first written above and shall continue for a period of **three (3) years**, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' written notice.

**9.2 Survival.** The obligations of confidentiality and non-circumvention shall survive termination or expiry for a period of **three (3) years** from the date of termination or expiry. The non-compete obligations under Clause 7 shall survive for a period of **six (6) months** from the date of termination or expiry. The non-solicitation obligations under Clause 6 shall survive for the period specified in that Clause.

**9.3 Return of Materials.** Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, or upon written request by Fabian, the Recipient shall promptly return or permanently destroy (and certify such destruction in writing) all Confidential Information and any copies thereof in their possession or control.

---

## 10. REMEDIES

**10.1 Injunctive Relief.** The Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to Fabian for which monetary damages alone may be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, Fabian shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and other equitable remedies without the requirement to post a bond or prove actual damages.

**10.2 Damages.** The foregoing shall not limit Fabian's right to claim damages, account for profits, or seek any other remedy available at law or in equity.

---

## 11. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**11.1 No Partnership or Employment.** Nothing in this Agreement shall create or be deemed to create any partnership, joint venture, employment, agency, or franchise relationship between the Parties.

**11.2 No Obligation to Proceed.** This Agreement does not obligate either Party to proceed with the Proposed Engagement, enter into any further agreement, or disclose any particular information.

**11.3 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior discussions, representations, and understandings relating to confidentiality in connection with the Proposed Engagement.

**11.4 Amendments.** This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

**11.5 Waiver.** No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver of that right.

**11.6 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable under applicable law, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be replaced with a valid provision that most closely reflects the original intent.

**11.7 Assignment.** Neither Party may assign or transfer any rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, save that Fabian may novate this Agreement to any incorporated entity as provided in Clause 8.

**11.8 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronic or digital counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall have the same legal effect as handwritten signatures under the Electronic Communications Act 2000.

---

## 12. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**12.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of **England and Wales**.

**12.2 Jurisdiction.** The Parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of **England and Wales** for the resolution of any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, save that either Party may apply to any court of competent jurisdiction for injunctive or other interim relief.

**12.3 Escalation.** Before commencing proceedings, the Parties agree to attempt to resolve any dispute in good faith through direct discussion within **twenty-one (21) days** of written notice of the dispute.
