Module 1
Groundstrokes + Serve Intro
Tuesday 3 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Free rally in a reduced court (yellow marks on the middle)
  • Keep the ball in play — slow and controlled
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand
Drill
  • 1 player in the back — cone behind to recover position, cone opposite for target direction
Rally
  • 2 in the back, forehand to forehand
  • Court reduced with yellow marks to slow pace and improve precision
Exercise 2 — Backhand
  • Same structure, backhand side
Second Part — Serve Intro + Game — 25 min
Serve
  • Rotate after 5 serves each — focus on getting the ball in, not power
  • Coach demonstrates grip and motion
Game 1
  • 4 players all in the back — coach feeds
  • Goal: reach 10 consecutive shots
Game 2
  • Same setup — each team has 5 lives; lose a life on a miss
If time
  • Tiebreak to 10, all from the back
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Free rally, full court — mix of groundstrokes, moving side to side
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand
Drill
  • 1 in the back — cone to recover, cone to guide target
Rally
  • Forehand to forehand — ball must bounce before the service line (deep and controlled)
Exercise 2 — Backhand
  • Same structure, backhand side
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve + Return
  • One serves, one returns — rotate every 5 serves
  • Focus on consistent motion and placement
Game 1
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — coach feeds to back
  • Goal: 10–15 consecutive shots
Game 2
  • Same — each team has 5 lives
If time
  • Tiebreak to 10, starting from the back
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court including overheads — arms loose, no bandeja yet
First Part — Glass Drills + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand + Side Glass
Drill
  • 1 in the back, forehand off side glass — cone for recovery, cone for target
Rally
  • 2 in the back — one plays down the line, other defends forehand off the glass
Exercise 2 — Backhand + Side Glass
Drill
  • Same structure, backhand side
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — net player directs consistently to the backhand
  • Back player aims down the line or cross
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — coach-fed
  • Net team controls — volleys with pressure; no passive play; net team must keep the initiative
Points 2
  • Back players must use the glass at least once per point
  • Win the rally off the glass = double points
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 2
Volleys + Net Game + Serve
Friday 8 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Reduced court rally — double-touch: players tap the ball once with the racket before hitting
  • Slows things down, builds control
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand Volley
Drill
  • 1 at the net — cone for recovery position, cone for target
Rally
  • 1 at the net vs 1 in the back — back hits groundstrokes; net plays volleys only
  • Volleys must land before the service line
Exercise 2 — Backhand Volley
  • Same structure, backhand side
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve
  • Focus on stance and toss — rotate after 5 serves each
Game 1
  • 2 at the net, 2 in the back — no lobs allowed, flat shots only
  • Goal: 10–15 consecutive shots
Game 2
  • 4 in the back — coach feeds; back pair goes to the net; other pair defends flat only
  • Tiebreak to 10
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally going to the net (no overhead smash yet)
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand Volley
Drill
  • 1 at the net — cone recovery + cone target
Rally 1
  • 1 net vs 1 back — groundstrokes vs volleys before service line
Rally 2
  • After the drill, progress to volley-to-volley between two net players
Exercise 2 — Backhand Volley
  • Same structure, backhand side
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve + Return
  • One serves, one returns to a target zone — rotate every 5
Game 1
  • 2 at the net, 2 in the back — no lobs; off-glass counts double
Game 2
  • 4 in the back — coach feeds; one side goes to net; other side defends flat
  • Tiebreak to 10
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overhead smashes
First Part — Volleys + Bajada Intro — 25 min
Exercise 1 — FH + BH Volley Sequence
Drill
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds one forehand, one backhand alternating
  • Then random — coach feeds without warning; player must read and react
Rally
  • 1 at the net, 1 in the back — back plays only low shots; net plays volleys only
  • Volleys land in a reduced zone
Exercise 2 — Mid-Height Bajada Intro
Drill
  • Player at the net performs a mid-height bajada — flat shot aimed at opponent's feet
  • After receiving a coach-fed ball off the glass — focus: separate from the ball, contact out front, aim low
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — back plays a fast lob
  • Net does defensive transition and plays mid-height bajada, then plays 2 straight shots and starts again with a lob
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Mid-height bajada — coach feeds a fast lob to net players, who must execute a bajada
  • Net players cannot move away from the net until the coach feeds
Points 2
  • Lob + go to net / Bajada — back team starts with a lob that must bounce after the service line, then transitions to the net
  • The pair starting at the net lets the ball bounce and plays a bajada to start their attack
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 3
Back Glass + Transition
Tuesday 10 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Rally in the middle, reduced court
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand off the Back Glass
Drill
  • Coach feeds a deep ball — player hits after the back glass (forehand)
  • Cone for recovery, cone for target — keep it easy; feed ball between player and the middle
Rally
  • 2 in the back — both aim deep, down the line; both defend using forehand off the glass
Exercise 2 — Backhand off the Back Glass
  • Same structure, backhand side
Second Part — Transition + Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 4 in the back — coach feeds deep; players return off glass and immediately move up to the net
  • Point continues from the net
Tactical 2
  • Same setup — win the rally off the glass = counts double
If time
  • King of the court
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally, going to the net included (no overhead)
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand off the Back Glass
Drill
  • Same as Beginners
Rally 1
  • 2 in the back — aim down the line, deep
Rally 2
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — net player directs deep; back player defends off glass and tries to pass
Exercise 2 — Backhand off the Back Glass
  • Same structure, backhand side
Second Part — Transition + Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — coach feeds deep to the back
  • Back defends off glass, then transitions; net lets the ball come — point starts once in position
Tactical 2
  • Lob + transition — back starts with a lob, follows to the net
  • Net lets the lob bounce, performs defensive transition, then builds
If time
  • King of the court
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads
First Part — Mixed Glass + Defensive Víbora — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Mixed Glass
Drill
  • Coach feeds to back glass, side glass, and corner alternately — player reads and adapts
Rally
  • 2 in the back, random glass mix
Exercise 2 — Rulo Intro + Defensive Víbora
Rulo Intro (brief — one drill only)
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds one high ball; player practises the rulo: grip, swing path, follow-through
  • Go slow — technique only
Defensive Víbora Drill
  • Player starts at the net, receives a deep lob from the coach
  • Performs a slower víbora to control direction and regain net position
  • Focus on footwork and control — not power
Second Part — Defensive Víbora Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Net players start by touching the net with their rackets
  • Coach feeds to defenders, who hit a deep lob — net players run back and hit a defensive víbora to regain position
Points 2
  • Same but defenders (after the lob) go to the net to challenge
  • Net players must defensive víbora and hold their ground
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 4
Lob + Bandeja / Víbora
Friday 15 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Reduced court — focus on height and depth of shots
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Lob
Drill
  • 1 at the net hitting lobs — cone to recover after each shot
  • Cone/zone on the other side to aim (deep, behind the service line)
Rally
  • 2 in the back — lob to lob; high and deep; goal: consistent depth, not speed
Exercise 2 — Bandeja/Víbora Intro
Drill
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds a lob; player plays bandeja (or víbora for more advanced beginners)
  • Cone for recovery, cone for target
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — back plays only low groundstrokes; net plays volleys only
  • Keep slow and controlled
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve
  • 5 serves each, rotate — focus on toss and contact point
Tactical 1
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — coach feeds lob to net; point starts with bandeja/víbora
Tactical 2
  • Coach feeds to the back — back starts with lob; net responds with bandeja/víbora
If time
  • Tiebreak to 10, all from the back
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court, going to the net with overhead included
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Lob
Drill
  • Same as Beginners
Rally 1
  • 2 in the back — lob to lob, high and deep
Rally 2
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — one lob, two low (back) vs one víbora, two volleys (net)
  • Structured rhythm
Exercise 2 — Bandeja/Víbora
Drill
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds lob; player executes bandeja/víbora; cone for recovery
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve + Return
  • Server aims to a zone — returner picks a target; rotate every 5
Tactical 1
  • Coach feeds lob to net — point starts with bandeja/víbora
Tactical 2
  • Back starts with lob; net responds with bandeja/víbora — full rally
Tactical 3
  • Back varies freely — lobs, low, glass; net must read and react; back cannot come to the net
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads
First Part — Bandeja/Víbora + Chiquita — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Only Lobs vs Only Víboras
Drill
  • Bandeja/Víbora drill — cone for recovery, cone for target
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — back plays only lobs; net plays only víboras
  • If back is under heavy pressure, they may play one low ball to reset before returning to lobs only
Exercise 2 — Chiquita/Lob Decision-Making
Drill
  • 1 in the back — coach feeds varied balls; player must play a chiquita before attempting a lob
  • If they skip straight to the lob, they stay in the back
Rally
  • 1 back, 1 at the net — same rule: back must play a chiquita before each lob attempt
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • 2 at the net vs 1 in the back — back plays long rallies varying pace until earning an easy ball to lob
  • Double point if lob bounces behind the yellow mark at the end of the court
Points 2
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — net touches the net; coach feeds lob to defenders
  • Net runs back, defensive víbora — defenders then go to the net to challenge
Points 3 (if time)
  • Win with víbora = 2 points; miss víbora = 2 points for rivals — builds execution under pressure
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 5
Mixed Review — Glass + Volleys + Serve
Tuesday 17 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Free rally, reduced court
First Part — Rotation Drill (2 Courts) — 25 min
Court 1
  • Forehand off the back glass drill + rally (2 in the back, forehand-to-forehand off glass)
Court 2
  • Forehand volley drill + rally (1 at net vs 1 in back, groundstrokes vs volleys, reduced area)
  • Teams of 2 rotate between courts — 4 rotations total
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve
  • Consistency — same motion every time; 5 serves each, rotate
King of the Court
  • Tiebreaks to 10 — losers rotate out; all start from the back; coach feeds first ball
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court going to the net (no overhead)
First Part — Rotation Drill (2 Courts) — 25 min
Court 1
  • Forehand + side glass drill + rally (2 in the back, forehand off glass, aim down the line)
Court 2
  • Backhand + side glass drill + rally (1 in the back, 1 at the net — low shots vs volleys)
  • 4 rotations, teams of 2 swap courts
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve + Return
  • One serves, partner returns to a target zone — rotate every 5
King of the Court
  • Glass counts double — tiebreaks to 10
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads
First Part — Direction Play + Víbora to Side Glass — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Víbora to Side Glass
Drill
  • 1 at the net, feet on the second stick — player performs a víbora aimed to the side glass
Points Drill
  • Coach feeds a lob — player must play víbora to the side glass before anything else
Rally
  • Only lobs + only víboras — back lobs only, net víboras only
  • Back may reset with one low ball if under pressure
Exercise 2 — Direction Play (2v1)
Rally
  • 2 at the net vs 1 in the back — back player hits two consecutive shots to the same opponent
  • Alternating target: 2 down the line, then 2 cross court
  • Net players: cross-court player volleys to the side glass; down-the-line player volleys to the middle
Second Part — Serve/Return Combinations — 25 min
Points 1
  • Serve + return + first volley — server follows the serve and plays the first volley at the returner's feet
  • Returner must play the return low
Points 2
  • Serve + attack against the returner — after the serve, the serving team must play against the player who returned
  • Until that player plays a lob or chiquita
Points 3
  • 2 at the net vs 1 in the back — consistency + decision-making
  • Back plays long rallies varying pace until earning a lob opportunity
  • Double point if lob lands behind the yellow mark
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 6
Transition — Defense to Attack
Friday 22 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Reduced court rally — footwork focus: recover position after every shot
First Part — Transition Intro — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Transition Intro
Drill
  • 1 in the back — coach feeds deep; player defends off glass and moves to the net
  • Stop and reset each time — practise the movement pattern, not the point
Rally
  • 2 in the back — one defends off glass, both transition together
Exercise 2 — Serve
  • Disguise direction slightly — pick left or right each time; 5 serves each, rotate
Second Part — Tactical Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 4 in the back — coach feeds deep; return off glass and transition to net as a team
Tactical 2
  • Same — the team that transitions first earns a point bonus if they win the rally
If time
  • Tiebreak to 10 from the back
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court, going to the net included
First Part — Transition — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Transition (Defense–Attack)
Drill
  • 1 in the back — coach feeds deep; player returns off glass and moves to net automatically
Rally 1
  • 2 in the back — defend + transition together
Rally 2
  • 2 back, 2 net — back lobs, transitions; net lets lob bounce, defensive transition, then builds
Exercise 2 — Serve + Return
  • Server picks direction — returner plays to a target zone; rotate every 5
Second Part — Tactical Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 4 in the back — return off glass + transition; win from net = counts double
Tactical 2
  • 2 back, 2 net — back lobs + transitions; net defends and recovers; first to 5 points
If time
  • King of the court
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads
First Part — Net Dominance — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Defensive Transition after a Passing Shot
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — back hits low; net player lets it pass and runs back (defensive transition), hits after glass; back moves to net
  • New back player hits 2 straight shots; net plays 2 volleys; net lets next ball pass again to repeat the cycle
Exercise 2 — Playing as a Team in Attack (2v1)
Rally
  • 2 at the net vs 1 in the back — net players must change direction through the middle on every attack shot
  • Back defends
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Volleys with pressure — net team keeps pressure continuously
  • If the defending pair reaches the net, they earn double points (the attacking team lost initiative)
Points 2
  • Keep pressure at net — if the net team wins the point in fewer than 4 hits, it counts double
Points 3
  • Defensive transition counts double — if the net players let the ball pass and win the point from the back, it counts double
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 7
Mixed Game — All Skills Combined
Tuesday 24 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Double-touch rally in a reduced court — tap the ball once before hitting; builds control
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — FH + BH Volley (Random)
Drill
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds randomly: forehand or backhand without warning; player reads and reacts
Rally
  • 1 at the net, 1 in the back — net mixes volleys; back aims low to make the volley harder
Exercise 2 — Lob + Volley Combo
Drill
  • 1 at the net hitting lobs — cone for recovery, cone for target (deep)
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — back: two low, one lob; net: two volleys, one bandeja
  • Structured rhythm
Second Part — Serve + Points — 25 min
Serve
  • 3 serves each — get it in with intention
Game 1
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — normal coach-fed points
Game 2
  • Tiebreak to 10, all from the back — win off glass = double points
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally, net approach included
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Glass + Volley
Drill
  • 1 in the back — coach feeds to back glass
Rally
  • 1 back, 1 at net — back uses glass when possible; net volleys and varies placement
Exercise 2 — Lob + Bandeja + Variation
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — back varies freely: lob, glass, low; net reads and responds
  • Back cannot come to the net
Second Part — Serve + Points — 25 min
Serve + Return
  • Server aims to a zone — returner picks target; rotate every 5
Game 1
  • 2 back, 2 net — win with glass from back = double; win with drop shot from net = double
  • Creates tactical decisions
King of the Court
  • Tiebreaks to 10
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads and overhead smashes
First Part — All Weapons — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Lob off Glass + Back Glass Crosscourt
Drill
  • 2 players at the back — one plays a lob off the double glass, returns with side steps around the marks
Points
  • Coach feeds from one side with angle to the back glass
  • Players must hit the first shot after the ball bounces off the glass
Exercise 2 — Two Low One Lob + One Lob One Low
Rally 1
  • Back plays two low then one lob; net plays two volleys then one víbora — structured rhythm
Rally 2
  • Back plays one lob then one low, alternating; net plays one víbora then one volley
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Two hits low not allowed — back team cannot play two consecutive low balls
  • Forces variation and creativity
Points 2
  • Make the ball bounce — back players create space with chiquita or lob; back must play the first ball low
  • Cannot come to the net unless they make the ball bounce — if they do, double point
Points 3
  • Transition Volley (Chiquita) — defending team receives a coach feed
  • They play a transition volley aimed at the feet of the net team, then the point is free
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 8
Match Play + King of the Court — Season Review
Friday 29 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Free rally, full court — players choose their warm-up: forehands, volleys, lobs
First Part — Quick Drill Review — 15 min
  • Coach picks 2 drills based on group needs
  • Suggestions: FH or BH off back glass (if transitions were rough)
  • FH/BH volley (if net play was weak)
  • Lob-to-lob rally (if height and depth need work)
Second Part — Match Play — 35 min
Serve
  • 3 serves each, quick round — same motion, pick a side
Match Play
  • Tiebreaks to 10 — coach feeds first ball; rotate pairs every tiebreak
King of the Court (last 15 min)
  • Winners stay on — everyone competes
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court, full warm up
First Part — Quick Drill Review — 15 min
  • Coach picks based on group needs
  • Suggestions: FH/BH off glass + transition
  • Volley sequence (FH then BH, random)
  • Lob + bandeja rally
Second Part — Match Play — 35 min
Serve + Return
  • 3 each — focus on placement
Match Play
  • Tiebreaks to 10 — glass counts double when winning a rally
King of the Court (last 15 min)
  • Tiebreaks to 7 — winners stay on
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads and smashes
First Part — Quick Tactical Review — 15 min
  • Coach picks 1–2 focused situations
  • Suggestions: defensive víbora execution; bajada under pressure
  • Chiquita/lob decision-making; serve + first volley to the feet
Second Part — Match Play — 35 min
Match 1
  • Tiebreaks to 7 — free play: all shots, no rules; full tactical
Match 2
  • Tactical game — each team devises a tactical plan before the set
  • Coaches try to identify the plan at the end
Final Tiebreak Option
  • Win the point with a víbora or bajada = counts double — ends the month on a high note
King of the Court (last 15 min)
  • All players compete — winners stay on
