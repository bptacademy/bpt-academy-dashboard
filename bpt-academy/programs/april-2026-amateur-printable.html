Module 1
Groundstrokes + Serve Intro
Tuesday 3 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Free rally in a reduced court (yellow marks on the middle)
  • Goal: keep the ball in play, slow and controlled
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand
Drill
  • 1 player in the back hitting forehands
  • Cone behind player → recover position after each shot
  • Cone on opposite side → guide target direction
Rally
  • 2 players in the back, forehand to forehand
  • Goal: long rallies, slow and in control
  • Court reduced with yellow marks
Exercise 2 — Backhand
Drill
  • Same structure as forehand drill but for backhand
Rally
  • 2 players in the back, backhand to backhand
  • Same reduced court setup
Second Part — Serve Intro + Game — 25 min
Serve Practice
  • 1–2 players serving from each side
  • Coach demonstrates grip and motion
  • Focus: get the ball in — no power needed
  • Rotate after 5 serves each
Game 1
  • 4 players all in the back — coach feeds the ball
  • Goal: reach 10 consecutive shots without missing
  • Communicate!
Game 2
  • Same setup — each team has 5 lives
  • Lose a life on every miss — first to 0 loses
If Time Left
  • Tiebreak to 10 — all players starting from the back
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Free rally, full court
  • Mix of groundstrokes, moving side to side
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand
Drill
  • 1 player in the back hitting forehands
  • Cone to recover position, cone to guide target
Rally
  • 2 players in the back, forehand to forehand
  • Ball must bounce before the service line (deep and controlled)
Exercise 2 — Backhand
Drill
  • Same structure but for backhand
Rally
  • 2 players in the back, backhand to backhand
  • Ball must bounce before the service line
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve Practice
  • Players serve — the other returns
  • Rotate after 5 serves
  • Focus: consistent motion and placement
Game 1
  • 2 players in the back, 2 at the net — coach feeds ball
  • Goal: 10–15 consecutive shots
Game 2
  • Same formation — each team has 5 lives
  • Lose a life on every miss
If Time Left
  • Tiebreak to 10, starting from the back
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court warm up including overhead smashes
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand + Side Glass
Drill
  • 1 player in the back — forehand plus immediate side glass recovery
  • Cone to mark recovery position
Rally
  • 2 players in the back
  • One plays down the line, the other defends using forehand off the glass
Exercise 2 — Backhand + Side Glass
Drill
  • Same structure but backhand, using the glass
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net
  • Net player directs ball to the backhand side
  • Back player aims down the line or cross
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — normal coach-fed points
  • Net team controls the point — no passive volleys
Points 2
  • Back players must use the glass at least once per point
  • Win off the glass → counts double
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 2
Volleys + Net Game + Serve
Friday 8 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Reduced court rally
  • Double-touch warm up: tap the ball once with the racket before hitting it over
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand Volley
Drill
  • 1 player at the net hitting forehand volleys
  • Cone behind to recover to the T position
  • Cone on the other side to guide where to aim
Rally
  • 1 at the net, 1 in the back
  • Volleys must land before the service line
Exercise 2 — Backhand Volley
Drill
  • Same structure but backhand volley
Rally
  • 1 at the net, 1 in the back — volleys only from the net player
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve Practice
  • Focus on stance and toss
  • Rotate after 5 serves each
Game 1
  • 2 at the net, 2 in the back — no lobs, only flat shots
  • Goal: 10–15 consecutive shots
Game 2
  • 4 players start in the back
  • Coach feeds to one side — those two go to the net
  • Other side plays flat only
  • Tiebreak to 10
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally including going to the net (no overhead smash yet)
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand Volley
Drill
  • 1 player at the net, forehand volley
Rally 1
  • 1 at the net, 1 in the back — net player volleys only (before service line)
Rally 2 (Progression)
  • Volley-to-volley between the two net players
Exercise 2 — Backhand Volley
Drill
  • Same structure but backhand
Rally 1
  • Groundstrokes vs volleys
Rally 2
  • Volley-to-volley after the drill
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve Practice
  • One serves, one returns — returner picks a side and commits
  • Rotate every 5
Game 1
  • 2 at the net, 2 in the back — no lobs
  • Off-glass counts double
Game 2
  • 4 players in the back — coach feeds one side, they go to the net
  • Tiebreak to 10
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court warm up with overhead smashes included
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand Volley + Drop Shot Intro
Drill
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds short balls
  • Player decides: volley away or drop shot
Rally
  • 1 at the net, 1 in the back — net player mixes volleys and drop shots
Exercise 2 — Backhand Volley + Drop Shot
Drill
  • Same as above but backhand side
Rally
  • Net player uses both backhand volley and drop shot variation
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Net team must use at least one drop shot per point
  • Drop shot winner counts double
Points 2
  • Tiebreak to 7 — net team cannot smash
  • Only volleys and drop shots allowed
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 3
Back Glass + Transition
Tuesday 10 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Rally in the middle, reduced court
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand off the Back Glass
Drill
  • Coach feeds a deep ball; player hits it after the back glass using a forehand
  • Cone to mark recovery position, cone for target direction
Rally
  • 2 in the back — both aim deep and down the line
  • Defending using forehand off the glass
Exercise 2 — Backhand off the Back Glass
Drill
  • Same structure but backhand
Rally
  • 2 in the back — both aim deep and down the line using backhand off the glass
Second Part — Transition + Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 4 in the back — coach feeds deep
  • Players return off the glass and move up to the net — point continues
Tactical 2
  • Same setup — glass win counts double
If Time Left
  • King of the court
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally, going to the net included (no overhead)
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Forehand off the Back Glass
Drill
  • Forehand off the glass — cone recovery + target
Rally 1
  • 2 in the back — aim down the line, deep
Rally 2 (Progression)
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net
  • Back player defends off the glass and tries to pass the net player
Exercise 2 — Backhand off the Back Glass
Drill
  • Same structure, backhand side
Rally 1
  • 2 in the back — down the line, deep, backhand off glass
Rally 2
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — back player defends backhand off glass
Second Part — Transition + Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 2 in the back, 2 at the net — back players defend off glass, then transition to the net
Tactical 2
  • Back players start with a lob, follow it to the net
  • Net players let the lob bounce — defensive transition
If Time Left
  • King of the court
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court warm up with overheads
First Part — Drill + Rulo Intro — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Side Glass + Back Glass (Mixed)
Drill
  • Coach feeds varied balls to side glass and back glass — player reads and adapts
Rally
  • 2 in the back — random mix of glass shots
Exercise 2 — Rulo Intro
Drill
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds a low ball
  • Player practises the rulo (wrist-heavy topspin smash, angled towards the side glass)
  • Focus: grip, swing path, follow-through
Rally
  • 1 in the back plays a lob; 1 at the net attempts the rulo
  • Back player practises defensive position after the rulo
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Back team must use the glass at least once per point
  • Net team practises rulo when a lob presents
Points 2
  • Tiebreak to 7 — rulo winner counts double
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 4
Lob + Bandeja/Vibora
Friday 15 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Reduced court rally — focus on height and depth of shots
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Lob
Drill
  • 1 at the net hitting lobs — cone to recover, cone/zone to aim for (deep, behind service line)
Rally
  • 2 in the back — lob to lob, high and deep
Exercise 2 — Bandeja/Vibora (Intro)
Drill
  • Coach feeds a lob — player practises bandeja or vibora
  • Cone for recovery and target
Rally
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net
  • Back player hits low groundstrokes; net player volleys only, slow and in control
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve Practice
  • 5 serves each — focus on toss and contact point
Tactical 1
  • Coach feeds lob to net players — they start with a bandeja or vibora
Tactical 2
  • Coach feeds to back players — they start with a lob
  • Net players respond with bandeja or vibora
If Time Left
  • Tiebreak to 10
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally — include going to the net with overhead
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Lob
Drill
  • 1 at the net hitting lobs — cone for recovery, cone/zone for target
Rally 1
  • 2 in the back — lob to lob, high and deep
Rally 2
  • 1 in the back, 1 at the net — low groundstrokes vs volleys (before service line)
Exercise 2 — Bandeja/Vibora
Drill
  • Coach feeds lob — player executes bandeja or vibora
Rally
  • Back player: one lob, two low shots
  • Net player: one vibora, two volleys — structured rhythm
Second Part — Serve + Game — 25 min
Serve + Return
  • One serves, one returns to a target side — rotate every 5
Tactical 1
  • Coach feeds lob to net team — point starts with bandeja/vibora
Tactical 2
  • Back team starts with a lob — net team responds
Tactical 3
  • Back player varies freely — lobs, low shots, glass
  • Net team must read and react
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads
First Part — Rulo + Drop Shot + Bandeja/Vibora — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Bandeja/Vibora vs Lob
Drill
  • Coach alternates lobs to different positions
  • Player plays bandeja or vibora depending on position and height
Rally
  • Back: one lob, two low. Net: one vibora, two volleys
Exercise 2 — Rulo + Drop Shot
Drill
  • High ball = rulo. Low ball = drop shot — player reads and decides quickly
Rally
  • Net player uses all weapons — bandeja, vibora, rulo (high balls), drop shot (low balls)
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Net team uses all tools — back team: glass win counts double
Points 2
  • Tiebreak to 7 — net team must earn position
  • Only stay at net if they won the previous rally
Last 10 min
  • King of the court

  • Full court rally going to the net (no overhead)
First Part — Rotation Drill — 25 min
Court 1

Court 2
  • Backhand + side glass drill + rally (1 in back, 1 at net, low shots vs volleys)
  • 4 rotations

Serve + Return

Advanced

  • Full court with overheads

Module 6
Transition — Defense to Attack
Friday 22 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Reduced court rally — focus on footwork, recover position after every shot
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Transition Intro
Drill
  • 1 in the back — coach feeds deep
  • Player defends off glass and moves to net — stop and reset each time
Rally
  • 2 in the back — one defends off glass, both support and try to finish at the net
Exercise 2 — Serve
  • Focus on picking a direction — 5 serves each, rotate
Second Part — Tactical Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 4 in the back — coach feeds deep
  • Return off glass, transition to net together
Tactical 2
  • First team to transition to net and win the rally gets a bonus point
If Time Left
  • Tiebreak to 10
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally, going to the net included
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Transition
Drill
  • 1 in the back — coach feeds deep
  • Return off glass, move to net immediately
Rally 1
  • 2 in the back — one defends off glass, both transition
Rally 2
  • 2 in the back, 2 at net — back starts with lob then transitions
  • Net lets lob bounce — defensive transition back
Exercise 2 — Serve + Return
  • Server picks direction — returner plays to a target zone
  • Rotate every 5
Second Part — Tactical Game — 25 min
Tactical 1
  • 4 in the back — return off glass, transition to net
  • Net win counts double
Tactical 2
  • 2 in the back, 2 at net — back team lob + transition
  • Net team defends and recovers — first to 5
If Time Left
  • King of the court
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads
First Part — Net Dominance — 25 min
Exercise 1
  • 2 at the net — coach feeds random balls (high, low, middle)
  • Communicate and play the right shot: volley, bandeja, vibora, rulo, or drop shot
  • Rally: 2 in back vs 2 at net — net team attacks, back team defends using glass, lobs, variation
Exercise 2 — Down the Line
  • 2 at net vs 1 in back — defender plays two shots to the same player alternating direction
  • Forces direction control under pressure
Second Part — Full Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Tiebreak to 7 — net team must win from the net
  • If pushed back, they lose a life
Points 2
  • Tiebreak to 7 — free play, all shots
  • Focus on reading the game
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
Module 7
Mixed Game — All Skills Combined
Tuesday 24 April 2026
Beginners
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Double touch rally in a reduced court
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Random Volley
Drill
  • 1 at the net — coach feeds randomly (forehand then backhand, no warning)
  • Player reads and reacts
Rally
  • 1 at net, 1 in back — net mixes volleys, back aims low
Exercise 2 — Lob + Volley Combo
Drill
  • 1 at net hitting lobs — cone for recovery and target
Rally
  • Back player: two low, one lob
  • Net player: two volleys, one bandeja
Second Part — Serve + Points — 25 min
Serve Practice
  • 3 serves each
Game 1
  • 2 in back, 2 at net — normal coach-fed points
Game 2
  • Tiebreak to 10 — all start from the back
  • Glass win counts double
Intermediate
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court rally, including net approach
First Part — Drill + Rally — 25 min
Exercise 1 — Glass + Volley
Drill
  • 1 in back — coach feeds to back glass, player defends and aims deep
Rally
  • 1 in back, 1 at net — back uses glass when possible, net varies placement
Exercise 2 — Lob + Bandeja + Variation
Rally
  • 1 in back, 1 at net — back varies freely (lob, glass, low groundstroke)
  • Net reads and responds — back cannot come to the net
Second Part — Serve + Points — 25 min
Serve + Return
  • Server aims to a zone — returner picks a target
  • Rotate every 5
Game 1

Game 2
  • King of the court — tiebreaks to 10
Advanced
Warm Up — 10 min
  • Full court with overheads and smashes
First Part — Rulo + Drop Shot + Tactical — 25 min
Exercise 1
  • Coach feeds 3 balls: lob (rulo), low (drop shot), medium (bandeja or vibora)
  • Player reads and executes quickly
  • Rally: 1 in back, 1 at net — net uses all weapons freely
Exercise 2
  • 2 at net vs 1 in back — back player alternates direction every two shots
  • Net pair communicates and covers both sides
Second Part — Tactical Points — 25 min
Points 1
  • Tiebreak to 7 — every rally must include at least one glass shot from the back team
  • If net wins without a glass shot — back team loses a bonus point
Points 2
  • Free tiebreak to 7 — full tactical, no rules
Last 10 min
  • King of the court
