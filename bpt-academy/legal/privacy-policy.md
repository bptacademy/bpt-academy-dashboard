## 3. What Personal Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

### 3.1 Account & Identity Data
- Full name
- Date of birth
- Email address
- Phone number
- Profile photograph or avatar (if uploaded)
- City of residence
- User role (student, coach, admin, parent)

### 3.2 Junior / Child Account Data
Where a parent or guardian registers on behalf of a child, we collect:
- Child's first and last name
- Child's date of birth / age
- Parent or guardian's full name, email address, and phone number

We do not collect any further personal data directly from children. All child accounts are created and managed by a parent or guardian.

### 3.3 Sporting & Performance Data
- Padel skill level and division
- Previous sporting experience
- Attendance records
- Training session progress and module completion
- Coaching notes
- Rankings and performance scores

### 3.4 Payment Data
- Payment records (amount, date, programme)
- Payment method (Stripe card payment or bank transfer confirmation)

We do **not** collect, store, or process your card number, bank account details, or sort code. Card payments are processed directly and securely by Stripe Payments UK Limited. Bank transfers are made directly from your bank to ours — we receive only confirmation that payment has been made.

### 3.5 Communications Data
- Messages sent via the in-app messaging system
- Announcements and notifications sent to your account

### 3.6 Media
- Training videos uploaded to and viewed on the Platform (hosted via Mux)
- Photographs uploaded to your profile or shared within the Platform

### 3.7 Technical Data
- Device type and operating system (collected by Expo/React Native)
- App usage data and session logs
- Push notification tokens

## 4. How We Collect Your Data

We collect personal data in the following ways:

- **Directly from you** — when you register, complete your profile, submit an application, make a payment, or communicate through the Platform
- **From a parent or guardian** — when they register a junior account on your behalf
- **Automatically** — through your use of the Platform (technical and usage data)
- **From third-party service providers** — our technology partners (listed in Section 8) may collect certain technical data directly when you use the Platform

## 5. Why We Process Your Data and Our Legal Basis

| Purpose | Legal Basis |
|---|---|
| Creating and managing your account | Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) UK GDPR) |
| Delivering coaching programmes and tracking progress | Performance of a contract |
| Processing payments and maintaining financial records | Performance of a contract; Legal obligation |
| Sending service notifications and programme updates | Performance of a contract; Legitimate interests |
| Safeguarding children and verifying parental consent | Legal obligation; Vital interests |
| Improving the Platform and fixing technical issues | Legitimate interests |
| Complying with legal and regulatory obligations | Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) UK GDPR) |
| Sending marketing communications (where consented) | Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) UK GDPR) |

**Children's data:** Where we process data relating to a child under 13, we rely on parental consent as our lawful basis. For children aged 13–17, we rely on the parental consent obtained at account registration.

## 6. How Long We Keep Your Data

| Data Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Account and identity data | Duration of account + 2 years after closure |
| Payment records | 7 years (UK tax law requirement) |
| Sporting and performance data | Duration of account + 1 year after closure |
| Communications and messages | 2 years from date of message |
| Child account data | Until the child reaches 18, or 1 year after account closure, whichever is sooner |
| Technical/usage data | 12 months on a rolling basis |

After the applicable retention period, your data will be securely deleted or anonymised.

---

## 7. Who Has Access to Your Data

Access to personal data within BPT Academy is strictly limited:

- **Super Administrators** — authorised BPT Academy personnel with platform-level access for operational and safeguarding purposes only
- **Coaches** — access only to data relating to students enrolled in their programmes (attendance, progress, coaching notes)
- **Parents / Guardians** — access only to their linked child's profile and progress data

We do **not** sell your personal data to any third party. We do not share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes.

## 10. Data Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously. We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures including:

- Encryption of data in transit (TLS/HTTPS)
- Encrypted data storage
- Role-based access controls limiting who can access your data
- Row-level security at the database level
- Regular security reviews

However, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your personal data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

If you suspect any unauthorised access to your account, please contact us immediately at office@bptacademy.uk.

## 11. Children's Privacy

We take the privacy and safety of children very seriously.

- Children under 18 may only use the Platform through an account created and managed by a parent or guardian
- We collect minimal data about children — only their name and date of birth
- Parents and guardians have the right to access, correct, and request deletion of their child's data at any time
- We do not knowingly collect personal data directly from children under 13
- If we discover that personal data has been collected from a child without proper parental consent, we will delete it immediately

If you are a parent or guardian and have concerns about your child's data, please contact us at office@bptacademy.uk.

## 12. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

- **Right of access** — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
- **Right to rectification** — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- **Right to erasure** ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your data in certain circumstances
- **Right to restrict processing** — ask us to limit how we use your data
- **Right to data portability** — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
- **Right to object** — object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
- **Rights related to automated decision-making** — we do not make solely automated decisions that significantly affect you

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at **office@bptacademy.uk**. We will respond within **30 days**.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at any time:
**Website:** ico.org.uk
**Helpline:** 0303 123 1113
