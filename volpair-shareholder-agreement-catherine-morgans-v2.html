5. Good Leaver / Bad Leaver

5.1 Good Leaver

A Shareholder is a "Good Leaver" if they cease to be involved with the Company due to:

  1. Death or permanent incapacity
  2. Termination by the Company without cause
  3. Mutual written agreement between all Shareholders
  4. Reduction in involvement or change of role
  5. Work commitments or personal circumstances
  6. Transition to an advisory, non-executive or consultancy capacity

A Good Leaver shall retain all vested shares and all unvested shares shall be transferred to the Company at nil value.

5.2 Bad Leaver

A Shareholder is a "Bad Leaver" only if they cease to be involved with the Company due to:

  1. Fraud
  2. Gross misconduct
  3. Wilful material breach of confidentiality obligations
  4. Deliberate unlawful conduct causing material harm to the Company

For the avoidance of doubt, a reduction in involvement, change of role, capacity constraints, cessation of active duties, or removal from an executive position shall not constitute a Bad Leaver event.

A Bad Leaver shall be required to transfer all shares (vested and unvested) to the Company or the Founder's nominee at the lower of: the original issue price; or fair market value as determined by the Company's accountants.

5.3 Share Retention Following Exit

Where Catherine Morgans ceases active involvement with the Company, reduces her involvement, changes role, or ceases to hold an executive position for any reason other than fraud, gross misconduct, wilful breach of confidentiality, or deliberate unlawful conduct causing material harm to the Company, she shall retain all vested shares and associated shareholder rights.

6. Pre-Emption Rights

6.1 Right of First Refusal

Before any Shareholder may transfer any shares to a third party, they must first offer those shares to the other Shareholders pro-rata to their existing holdings, at the same price and on the same terms as proposed to the third party.

6.2 Acceptance Period

The non-selling Shareholders shall have 30 days from receipt of written notice to accept the offer in full. If not accepted within 30 days, the selling Shareholder may proceed with the transfer to the third party at no less than the offered price, within 60 days thereafter.

6.3 New Share Issuances

Any new shares issued by the Company shall first be offered to existing Shareholders on a pro-rata basis before being offered to third parties, on the same terms.

7. Drag-Along Rights

7.1 Drag-Along

If Shareholders holding more than 75% of the total issued shares accept a bona fide written offer from an unconnected third party to acquire all of the shares in the Company, they may by written notice require all remaining Shareholders to sell their shares to that third party on the same terms and at the same price per share.

8. Tag-Along Rights

8.1 Tag-Along

If any Shareholder proposes to sell shares representing more than 20% of the total issued shares to a third party, each other Shareholder shall have the right (exercisable within 21 days of written notice) to require that the proposed buyer also purchases a pro-rata portion of their shares on the same terms.

9. Intellectual Property

9.1 Company Ownership

All intellectual property created by any Shareholder in connection with the Company's business — including but not limited to the Platform, code, designs, brand assets, data, algorithms, and know-how — is and shall remain the exclusive property of the Company.

9.2 Assignment

Each Shareholder hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all such intellectual property and agrees to execute any further documents reasonably required to perfect such assignment.

9.3 Pre-existing IP

Any intellectual property owned by a Shareholder prior to the Commencement Date and used in the Company's business shall be licensed to the Company on a royalty-free, perpetual basis unless otherwise agreed in writing.

10. Confidentiality

10.1 Obligation

Each Shareholder shall keep strictly confidential all Confidential Information, including:

  1. The Company's business plans, technology, finances, and strategy
  2. Details of customers, partners, and suppliers
  3. The existence and terms of this Agreement
  4. Any information designated as confidential by any party

10.2 Permitted Disclosure

A Shareholder may disclose Confidential Information only:

  1. To their professional advisers under equivalent confidentiality obligations; or
  2. As required by law or regulation, provided that the disclosing party gives prior written notice where lawfully possible.

10.3 Duration

This obligation survives termination of this Agreement for a period of 3 years.

11. Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation

11.1 During Engagement

During the period in which a Shareholder is actively engaged with the Company, they shall not without prior written Board consent:

  1. Be employed by, consult for, or hold a material interest in any business that directly competes with the Company's Platform
  2. Solicit or divert any customer, partner, or investor away from the Company

11.2 Post-Termination Restrictions

For a period of 12 months following cessation of active engagement with the Company, a Shareholder shall not:

  1. Establish, join, or materially assist any directly competing business in the padel player matching or social sports application space
  2. Solicit, recruit, or endeavour to poach any employee, contractor, or key commercial partner of the Company

11.3 Reasonableness

The parties acknowledge that the restrictions in this Clause are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests. If any restriction is found to be unenforceable, it shall be reduced to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable.

12. Future Employment, Consultancy or Payroll Arrangements

12.1

Any future employment, consultancy, salary, fee arrangement, payroll position, or increase in operational responsibilities shall be governed by a separate written agreement and shall not affect existing share ownership unless expressly agreed in writing by all parties.

13. Dividends and Distributions

13.1 Dividends may only be declared by a resolution of the Board.

13.2 All dividends and distributions shall be paid pro-rata to shareholdings at the time of declaration.

13.3 The Founder may by written notice defer his dividend entitlement in whole or in part to fund Company operations.

14. Dispute Resolution

14.1 Negotiation

The parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising under this Agreement by direct negotiation within 30 days of written notice of the dispute.

14.2 Mediation

If unresolved after 30 days, the dispute shall be referred to a mutually agreed independent mediator before any legal proceedings are commenced.

14.3 Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

15. General Provisions

15.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties in relation to its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral.

15.2 Amendments. No amendment to this Agreement shall be binding unless made in writing and signed by all Shareholders.

15.3 Waiver. No failure or delay by any party to exercise any right or remedy shall constitute a waiver of that right or remedy.

15.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

15.5 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by: (a) Email with read receipt; or (b) Recorded postal delivery — to the addresses set out at the head of this Agreement, or such other address as a party may notify in writing.

15.6 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be an original, and together they shall constitute one instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid.
